Daniel Nima Moattar, reporter-researcher

I am an independent investigative journalist in Bridgeport, Chicago, with work in The Guardian, The Nation, Salon, and other outlets.
I use interviews and public records, especially FOIA, to report public and private malfeasance. I have too many tabs open.

[reporting]

    I've investigated conservative think tanks, Chicago school closures, and violence in prisons. I also report on:
  • labor (Salon, The Nation),
  • business (In These Times),
  • immigration (The Guardian, The American Prospect),
  • education (South Side Weekly),
  • and movement politics (VICE, The Nation), nationally and in Chicagoland.

[research]

    I've fact-checked print and broadcast reporting for dozens of publications, and researched for newsrooms, non-profits, and media outlets.

  • For The Atavist, I've checked 10- to 20,000-word features on wrongful convictions, pediatric neurology, and Cold War crises.
  • For New York Public Radio, I worked on the debut episode of More Perfect, Radiolab's series on the U.S. judiciary.
  • I fact-check Life Inside, an award-winning series on incarceration at The Marshall Project.
